## Adjacent and reference projects

- **AgMIP** (Agricultural Model Intercomparison and Improvement Project): agmip.org. Started 2010. Multi-institutional. Direct organizational analogue for 3MIP.
- **ISIMIP** (Inter-Sectoral Impact Model Intercomparison Project): isimip.org. Coordinated by PIK Potsdam. Direct organizational analogue.
- **CMIP6** (Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6): the gold standard intercomparison framework in climate science. Reference for "what good intercomparison looks like."
- **COASTMOVE**: coastmove.wordpress.com. Tierolf's home project. Complementary, not competing.
- **HABITABLE** (EU Horizon 2020): adjacent climate migration consortium, more policy and empirics focused.
- **MISTY**, **PRISE**: smaller adjacent consortia.
- **World Bank Groundswell reports** (Rigaud, de Sherbinin, Jones, Bergmann, et al., 2018; 2021): top-down country-level migration projections. 3MIP outputs would feed bottom-up alternatives.
- **UN Global Centre for Climate Mobility (GCCM)**: policy interface partner for the founding workshop.

## Anti-facts (do not state these — they have appeared on AI-generated drafts of this material and are wrong)

- 3MIP is **not** an NSF-funded project as of May 2026. NSF RCN application is planned, status not public.
- 3MIP is **not** affiliated with the IPCC.
- 3MIP is **not** a UN program. The UN GCCM co-sponsored the founding workshop only.
- The founding workshop was **not** held at Cornell. It was held at Princeton C-PREE.
- Bell is **no longer at NYU**. He moved to Cornell in July 2024.
- The first benchmark case is **coastal Bangladesh specifically**, not "South Asia" or "the Ganges Delta" generally.
- 3MIP does **not** produce migration forecasts. It coordinates intercomparison among independent modeling teams. The teams produce outputs; 3MIP synthesizes.
- The *Climatic Change* topical collection is **planned, not open**. Springer has not posted a formal call as of May 2026.

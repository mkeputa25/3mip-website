# 3MIP Website — Copy Guide

This document specifies the voice, register, and editorial rules for all written content on the 3MIP website. It exists because voice consistency across nine pages requires concrete examples, not abstractions.

When in doubt, follow the examples. When the examples don't cover a case, default to: short, specific, active voice, citable.

## Voice

**Academic but readable. Direct. Specific. Confident without being marketing-aspirational.**

The reader is, by ranked priority: an active climate-migration modeler, a funder program officer, a policymaker, or an adjacent academic. None of them needs to be sold. They need to be informed.

The site speaks as the project: "3MIP brings together..." not "We bring together..." The first-person plural appears only when explicitly representing the leadership (e.g., in the About page governance section). Avoid the first person where the third person is more accurate.

## Register

- Active voice over passive. "3MIP coordinates modelers" not "Modelers are coordinated by 3MIP."
- Specific over abstract. "175.7 million people" not "a large population."
- Concrete over conceptual. "Bell developed the MIDAS agent-based framework" not "Bell has worked on innovative modeling approaches."
- Plain over latinate. "Use" not "utilize." "Help" not "facilitate." "Show" not "demonstrate."
- Verifiable claims only. If you cannot link a citation, do not make the claim.

## Sentence length

Mix. Most sentences should be ≤25 words. Vary deliberately. Short sentences land harder. Long sentences carry nuance.

Example:

> 3MIP is a community of modelers who agree to apply their approaches to a shared benchmark case. The first case is coastal Bangladesh.

vs.

> 3MIP, the Mobility and Migration Modeling Intercomparison Project, represents a coordinated effort by leading researchers worldwide to bring together diverse modeling approaches to climate migration in order to advance the field through collaborative benchmarking.

The first works. The second is what we are avoiding.

### Banned opening patterns

These openings are forbidden on every page:

- "In today's rapidly changing world..."
- "Climate migration is one of the defining challenges of our time..."
- "As the climate crisis intensifies..."
- "[X] has emerged as a critical issue..."
- "In recent years..."
- "Researchers around the world..."
- "Welcome to 3MIP."
- "We are a collaborative initiative dedicated to..."

These openings are forbidden because every academic site uses them, they say nothing, and they take 25 words to do it.

### Banned closing patterns

- "Together, we can..."
- "Join us in..."
- "Stay tuned for..."
- "Get in touch to learn more..."
- "We are excited to..."

Closings should end with a concrete next action or a verifiable fact. Not a sentiment.

### Banned structural patterns

- **Hedge stacks**: "may potentially help to better understand..." — pick one verb.
- **Vague quantification**: "a number of," "various," "several," "a range of" — use a real number or delete.
- **Throat-clearing**: "It is important to note that..." — note it without saying you're noting it.
- **Self-praise**: "Our distinguished team..." — let the credentials speak.
- **Tautological intensifiers**: "extremely important," "very critical," "absolutely essential" — pick a strong noun and drop the intensifier.

## Examples — page-level

### BAD: hero copy

> 3MIP is a pioneering, world-class collaborative initiative leveraging cutting-edge modeling approaches to revolutionize our understanding of climate migration in this rapidly changing world.

Why it fails: every banned word, no specifics, says nothing.

### GOOD: hero copy

> 3MIP coordinates climate-migration modelers around shared benchmark cases. The first case is coastal Bangladesh, where five model architectures will project the same question and the results will be synthesized for the iEMSs 2026 Dublin session.

Why it works: states what the project is, what's happening now, when results come.

### BAD: About page opening

> Climate migration is one of the defining challenges of our time. As temperatures rise and seas encroach, communities around the world face unprecedented displacement. 3MIP was founded by a passionate team of researchers committed to addressing this critical issue through innovative, robust modeling approaches.

Why it fails: hits five banned patterns in three sentences. Says nothing about what 3MIP does.

### GOOD: About page opening

> 3MIP grew out of a workshop at Princeton's Center for Policy Research on Energy and the Environment in September 2024, where more than thirty researchers from three continents agreed that climate-migration modeling needed what crop modeling and cross-sectoral impacts modeling already had: a coordinated intercomparison.

Why it works: dated, specific, names the institutional context, sets up the AgMIP/ISIMIP analogy without forcing it.

### BAD: Case page opening

> Bangladesh represents an incredibly important case study for understanding the future of climate migration. With its vulnerable coastal regions and growing population, the country faces transformative challenges that require innovative solutions.

Why it fails: "incredibly important," "vulnerable," "transformative," "innovative solutions" — marketing register on a research page.

### GOOD: Case page opening

> Bangladesh has 175.7 million people, much of it living in a low-lying delta. Bell et al. (2021) projected that migration toward Bangladesh's coast — not away from it — will continue through 2100 under all sea-level rise scenarios studied. The empirical density and policy stakes make it the right place for a first benchmark.

Why it works: specific number with citation, defends the case selection, links to the empirical literature.

### BAD: Participate FAQ

> Q: How can I join 3MIP?
>
> A: We are excited to welcome new modelers! Joining is easy — just fill out our user-friendly registration form and we'll be in touch soon to onboard you onto our innovative platform.

Why it fails: "excited," "user-friendly," "innovative platform," reads like a SaaS signup page.

### GOOD: Participate FAQ

> Q: How do I join 3MIP?
>
> A: Fill out the registration form on this page. You'll receive an email with access to the curated input dataset and a participant handbook within five working days. Joining commits you to attending two webinars per year and submitting at least one output by the end of 2027 — see "What participation involves" above for full expectations.

Why it works: concrete process, concrete timeline, concrete commitment.

## When the field disagrees with itself

Migration modeling produces divergent projections. That divergence is 3MIP's reason for existing, not something to hide.

Write the disagreement plainly:

> Gravity-model projections for coastal Bangladesh migration to 2050 differ from agent-based projections by an order of magnitude. 3MIP surfaces this gap rather than averaging it away.

Not:

> While there are some differences in modeling approaches, the field is making important progress toward consensus.

The second sentence is a lie of omission.

## When uncertainty is fundamental

For uncertain claims, name the uncertainty and proceed:

> Sea-level rise projections for the Bangladesh coast vary by a factor of three across IPCC scenarios. The benchmark case uses SSP2-4.5 as the baseline and SSP5-8.5 as a sensitivity scenario.

Not:

> Climate change presents complex challenges for coastal populations.

## Citation conventions

In-prose citations: author-year format, parenthesized.

> Bell et al. (2021) projected continued coastal migration through 2100.

Full citation appears in:
- The Outputs page (for 3MIP-affiliated papers)
- The Data page (for dataset citations)
- The Case page footnotes (for the literature supporting the case selection)

DOI links: prefer DOI URLs (`https://doi.org/...`) over publisher URLs.

External links to working papers, preprints, or news items: open in same tab. Use the discreet ↗ external link glyph.

## Person references

- First reference: full name + primary affiliation. "Andrew Bell (Cornell)" not just "Andrew" and not "Dr. Andrew R. Bell, Ph.D."
- Subsequent references on the same page: surname only. "Bell" or "Bell and Best."
- Honorifics: avoid. "Bell" not "Professor Bell" or "Dr. Bell." Exception: Press releases or formal letters, neither of which appears on this site.
- Order of co-lead listing: alphabetical by surname (Bell, Best, Tierolf). Use this order consistently across the site unless context requires otherwise (e.g., a chronological narrative).

## Numbers, dates, units

- Numbers under ten: spelled out in prose ("six modelers"). Ten and above: numerals ("30 researchers").
- Exception: numbers with units use numerals ("4 hours," "2 km").
- Statistics and citations always use numerals ("175.7 million people").
- Dates: long format in prose ("September 25–27, 2024"). Short format in metadata ("2024-09-25").
- Date ranges use an en dash, not a hyphen ("2025–2050").
- Times: ISO 8601 with timezone for events ("2026-07-15T14:00Z"). Display with timezone abbreviation ("3 PM UTC").
- Currency: spell out first, abbreviate after if repeated. "€2,499,608.75" for grant amounts (per CORDIS).
- Avoid percentages without a base. "30% increase" is meaningless without "from what."

## Headings

- Sentence case, not Title Case. "About 3MIP" not "About 3MIP." Wait — those look identical. Let me try again. "Why Bangladesh" not "Why Bangladesh." Hmm. Let me be explicit:
  - Sentence case: only the first word and proper nouns capitalized. "Why Bangladesh is the first case."
  - NOT title case: where every major word is capitalized. "Why Bangladesh Is the First Case."
  - Use sentence case.
- One `<h1>` per page. The page title.
- `<h2>` for top-level sections. `<h3>` for subsections. Do not skip levels.
- Avoid heading-stacking: no `<h2>` immediately followed by `<h3>` with no content between.
- Headings are signposts, not advertisements. "What we expect of participants" not "Become Part of a World-Changing Initiative."

## Buttons and CTAs

- Verb-first. "Register" not "Registration." "Read the workshop summary" not "Workshop summary."
- Specific over generic. "Register for the Bangladesh case" not "Get started" or "Learn more."
- Match the button to the next action. If a button says "Register," it goes to a registration form, not a marketing page.

## Empty states

When a section has no content yet (Working papers, future cases, the *Climatic Change* collection):

- Be honest. Do not pretend content exists.
- Explain what will appear there. "Working papers from participating teams will appear here as they are released."
- Give a return signal. "Subscribe to the news RSS feed for updates" or "Next anticipated release: late 2026."

Empty states are not failures. Dishonest empty states are failures.

## Accessibility-aware copy

- Avoid directional cues that assume sight ("see the chart to the left"). Use "see the figure below" or describe the chart in text.
- Avoid color-only references ("the red line shows..."). Always pair color with shape, label, or pattern.
- Link text is descriptive on its own. "Read the dataset documentation" not "Click here."
- Avoid jargon without a quick gloss the first time it appears. "Agent-based model (ABM)" on first use; "ABM" after.

## SEO meta descriptions

Per-page meta descriptions, ≤155 characters, written for humans first, search engines second.

Format: subject + verb + value. End with a concrete noun, not a sentiment.

Examples:

- Home: "3MIP coordinates climate-migration modelers around shared benchmark cases. First case: coastal Bangladesh. Synthesis at iEMSs Dublin, July 2026."
- About: "3MIP grew out of a 2024 Princeton workshop. Co-leads at Cornell, Ohio State, and VU Amsterdam. Advisory committee includes Oppenheimer and de Sherbinin."
- Case: "Coastal Bangladesh is 3MIP's first benchmark case. 175.7 million people, sea-level rise projections, agent-based and gravity model comparisons."
- Participate: "Modelers using ABM, gravity, radiation, IAM, or ML approaches can join 3MIP's first benchmark case. Registration is open."

## What this guide does not cover

- **Translation**: the site is English-only in v1. Standard Albanian or other languages are v2 scope.
- **Press releases**: 3MIP does not issue press releases. Mario can draft them separately; they do not appear on the site.
- **Social media voice**: the site does not have social channels in v1. If added, they require their own guide.
- **Long-form working papers**: when working papers are added in the future, they follow the journal style of their target publication, not this guide.

When in doubt, choose the shorter, more specific, more verifiable phrasing. When the choice is between a sentence that sounds smart and a sentence that says something true, choose true.

Dr. Khandaker Jafor Ahmed was recently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the
University of Michigan's School for Environment and Sustainability, and
previously at Georgetown University's Institute for the Study of International
Migration, where he worked on the Bangladesh Environment and Migration Survey
(BEMS). His research examines climate vulnerability, migration, and health
outcomes in low- and middle-income countries, with particular focus on how
environmental stressors affect population displacement and vulnerable
communities. He earned his PhD in Environmental Studies from the University of
Adelaide (2022). With 30 peer-reviewed publications, his work integrates
quantitative and qualitative methods to understand climate-demographic
relationships.
